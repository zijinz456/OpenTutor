## Top 3 Marketing Highlights This Week

The 2026-07-14 community post drafts and the Week 28 build-in-public content provide strong material. These are the three most compelling angles for social posts and demos in the coming week:

### 1. Behavioral Block Unlock Overlay — Progressive Disclosure Without a Paywall
`apps/web/src/lib/block-system/feature-unlock.ts` (~75 lines) + `block-wrapper.tsx`. The 2026-07-14 HN draft explains the design clearly: blocks render behind frosted glass with visible progress counters ("2/3 documents", "47/50 attempts"). Locked state shows what students are working toward, not a wall they can't see past. The unlock conditions are semantically grounded — the Knowledge Graph requires 3+ source documents because a graph from one PDF is trivially small; the Forecast requires 50+ attempts because fewer makes trajectory prediction noise.

**Demo angle:** Screen-record the transition from "47/50 attempts" overlay to the Forecast block fully unlocking. Short, concrete, no technical explanation needed. This is the most immediately visual feature in the codebase.

**Post angle:** Lead with the anti-pattern ("most tools either front-load everything or gate with subscriptions") then show the third option. The implementation detail (75 lines, pure function, localStorage-backed) makes it engineering-credible for HN and r/LocalLLaMA.

### 2. Two-Tier LLM Routing — 3B for Decisions, 7B+ for Actions
The ambient monitor uses llama3.2:3b (~0.8s warm) for lightweight JSON decisions and routes content generation to `ModelTier.FRONTIER` (7B+, ~6–8s on M2 Pro). The user approval gate (Tier 2 action) fires before any expensive generation, decoupling decision latency from action latency. The r/LocalLLaMA post in the 2026-07-14 drafts includes measured benchmarks that make this concrete.

**Demo angle:** Show the "I think you'd benefit from AI-generated notes" approval card appearing without a UI freeze, then show the generation running after approval.

**Post angle:** The pattern — lightweight model observes, heavier model acts, user approves before execution — is broadly applicable to local LLM app design. Frame it as a design pattern, not just an OpenTutor feature.

### 3. LECTOR Knowledge-Graph-Aware Review Queue vs. Anki's Simple Due Count
`apps/api/services/lector.py` (344 lines). The r/Anki post in the 2026-07-14 drafts explains the distinction clearly: Anki's due count is time-based; LECTOR's is prerequisite-ordered, confusion-pair-grouped, and cognitive-load-gated. The Daily Digest block shows the filtered count alongside mastery percentage, framing it as "context for the session" rather than a score to maximize.

**Demo angle:** Open the Daily Digest block and walk through what "23 due" means versus what Anki would show for the same deck — the ordering is different even if the number is the same.

**Post angle:** Strongest in r/Anki and r/Zettelkasten communities. Spaced repetition users care deeply about scheduling nuance; prerequisite-ordering is a gap in every mainstream SRS tool.
